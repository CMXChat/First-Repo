Rules of the Road

Public data only. We operate with open sources, authorized tools, and consented access. No intrusion, no doxxing, no social engineering for secrets. If it is gated or private, do not touch it.

Reproducibility. Cite sources, timestamp everything, log queries. Screenshots with URLs, archive links, and hashes where relevant.

Safety. Personal info is handled with care, victims first. If a target is a private person, treat with extra caution. Escalate threats to CXI, and document calmly.

Report upward instantly if you detect:
  • Threats to life, minors, or physical safety, notify CXI immediately.
  • Coordinated harassment or stalking patterns, spoofing, and real world escalation.
  • Signals of compromised systems, impersonation of officials, or extortion attempts.

OSINT Playbook

Pillars. People, Platforms, Infrastructure, and Media. Every task sits somewhere on that map. Work in layers, baseline, pivot, verify, package.

Task framing, before you touch the keyboard
  • Objective: What outcome matters, identify, attribute, map assets, timelines, threat pattern, or reputational triage.
  • Scope: Public sources only, within timeframe X, in languages A and B.
  • Exit: When do you stop, confidence threshold, time budget, decision deadline.
Evidence discipline
  • Every claim gets, URL plus timestamp plus archive link plus screenshot if likely to be edited or deleted.
  • Keep a running log, search strings, filters, site queries, usernames tested, hashes computed.
  • Prefer primary sources, treat reposts and anonymous claims as rumor until verified.
Attribution sanity check
  • Identity is not the same as a handle. Prove control across time, posts, cross links, unique photos, old caches.
  • Beware edited audio or video and staged leaks. Cross validate with independent traces.
  • If harm could follow, reduce precision and escalate to CXI for red team review.

Surface Web TTPs

Fast pivots you will use daily. Tune to your target with dates, site scoping, and minus terms.

Search patterns
  • Exact phrases: "full name" "city", "email" filetype:pdf
  • Site scope: site:linkedin.com/in "Name" "City", site:reddit.com u/handle
  • Cache and archives: web archives, search engine caches, and result view source.
  • Local media: regional outlets and community boards beat national sources for first sightings.
Contact intelligence
  • Email footprints, gravatar hash, public PGP keys, forum posts.
  • Phone digits as exact searches, look for country patterns, marketplace listings, resume PDFs.
  • Cross link by recovery emails shown partially, profile reuse, and unique avatars.
Time and place
  • Reverse image for landmarks, street furniture and signage narrow locations quickly.
  • Event pages, meetups, and livestream chat logs create reliable timelines.

Social and People OSINT

Identity graph. Build a small graph, person to emails to usernames to domains to devices to locations. The trick is reuse, people reuse handles, avatars, phrases, bios, and time of day posting habits.

Handle and profile triage
  • Check standard platforms, GitHub, Reddit, X, Instagram, YouTube, TikTok, Keybase, Steam, for the same handle. Capture profile IDs, not just vanity URLs.
  • Look for cross posted links, personal site, Linktree, Carrd, itch.io, code repos.
  • Language and emoji patterns are fingerprints, same with typos and punctuation style.
Community and context
  • Which subreddits, Discords, or forums, who replies regularly, map influence, who amplifies who.
  • Use time windows, compare posting hours to likely timezones. Holidays and sports talk give away countries.
Harassment diagnostics, for CXI handoff
  • Pattern stalking, repeated contact after blocks, burner rotation, identity theft, or impersonation of officials.
  • Escalation markers, mentions of minors, doxx threats, real world photos, men are coming language.
  • On handoff include, timeline bullets, call screenshots, numbers used, links to posts, and your brief assessment.

Domain, IP, Infrastructure

Ownership and routing tell stories. Track DNS history, certificates, hosting changes, and ASNs. Pivot from domains to IPs and back, watch for clumsy opsec like shared analytics IDs and reused CDN buckets.

Quick path
  1. Resolve DNS, A, AAAA, MX, NS, TXT. Note SPF, DKIM, DMARC for mail posture.
  2. Pull certs, crt.sh, for subdomains and previous names.
  3. Check passive DNS and scanners, urlscan, VirusTotal, Censys, Shodan, for exposed services.
  4. ASN and route history, BGP tools, to identify providers and geo drift.
Risk hints
  • Disposable hosting, frequent nameserver churn, typo squat clusters, and recently issued certs on new domains.
  • Login portals exposed to the open internet with default banners, capture versions, not just screenshots.

Images, Video, and Audio

Prove authenticity before you reason from it. Reverse search, frame grab, and metadata. For audio, assume edits are possible, look for background room tone mismatches and compression artifacts between segments.

Workflow
  1. Hash and filename capture. If EXIF exists, extract and map GPS carefully, never publish exact home coordinates.
  2. Reverse, multiple engines, drop in search on each, compare near duplicates and earliest sightings.
  3. Video, key frame every two or three seconds for search, match clothing, weather, and sun angle for date and time checks.

Automation and Speed

Stay client side where possible. Small scripts, saved searches, keyboard macros. Automate repetitive pivots but keep a human in the loop for interpretation and ethics.

Saved queries
  • Maintain a personal library of site: and filetype: searches for your verticals.
  • Keep collections of link hubs for email, username, domain, IP checks so you can open batches fast.
Notes and reproducibility
  • Use consistent headings, Objective, Steps, Findings, Confidence, Next actions.
  • Always include a How to reproduce block at the end for another analyst.

CXI Case Files, patterns we care about

Scope. CXI handles coordinated harassment, identity theft, spoofing operations, and real world escalation tied to online activity. Operators collect public evidence and escalate when thresholds are met.

Pattern, coordinated harassment and identity theft
  • Signals, rotating burner accounts, impersonation of friends or family, edited media, threats mentioning addresses or children.
  • What we document, numbers or handles used, timestamps, call or text screenshots, URLs, and a clear timeline of escalation.
  • Why it matters, public documentation preserves the truth and prepares legal handoff.
Note: When summarizing public allegations, attribute to the published report and avoid asserting unverified claims as facts.
Echo feed to training data

Echo is the live memory, a running log of threats and incidents, archived for training and forensics. It turns chaotic inputs into organized, searchable signal for future analysts.

Operations and Reporting

Deliverables. Executive one pager, investigative brief, or longform report. Each has a different audience, the core evidence is shared and reproducible.

Executive one pager, template
  • Situation: one paragraph in plain language.
  • Key Findings: three to five bullets with links and timestamps.
  • Risk: what could happen next, who is impacted.
  • Actions: concrete steps, contact, platform reports, legal options.
Investigative brief, template
  • Objective and scope, background, methods, findings per source, attribution analysis, confidence, and appendix with raw links.
  • Include archive URLs and hashes for any media likely to be removed.
Quality bar
  • Every claim tied to evidence. Distinguish observed vs inferred.
  • Bias control, explicitly list alternative explanations you ruled out.
  • Redaction, remove sensitive home addresses for private individuals unless there is an urgent safety reason to include for law enforcement.

  • Passive DNS: historical resolutions cached by third parties.
