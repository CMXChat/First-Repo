Executive overview

Ovaro is built to be a calm, technical partner for founders and marketing leads. To match that, the site needs a simple structure, one clear intent per page, and proof that shows we can actually ship. We do this by building a services spine, connecting it to the right use cases, and wiring tracking so every visit has a job.

Plan to move first

  • Launch a focused Services hub with Web Design & Development and SEO as the core entry points, with Paid campaigns used to test and accelerate the best ideas.
  • Give each core service its own pillar page and break out high value variants, like Landing Pages and SEO for Startups.
  • Bring simple Pricing, a Discovery Call page, and a short “Why Ovaro” story online so late-stage visitors have a clean next step.
  • Add three to four starter industry pages that line up with current Ovaro targets, such as Startups & SaaS, Healthcare, Local Services, and Professional Services.

Why this order helps

  • We lead with what Ovaro sells now instead of listing everything we could do later.
  • Single-intent pages for core services line up with how people search, which lifts relevance and click through.
  • Pricing, process, and case proof remove friction and let serious buyers move straight into a call.

Quick audit and setup

Analytics and search setup

  • Wire Google Analytics 4 and Tag Manager for form submissions, click-to-call, email clicks, and calendar bookings.
  • Verify the primary domain in Search Console, submit a clean XML sitemap, and remove stray test or staging URLs.
  • Tag key funnels, for example Home → Services → Discovery Call, so we can see how people actually move.

Technical hygiene

Keep one canonical domain and protocol, enforce HTTPS, and avoid duplicate routes for the same content. This keeps crawling efficient and avoids splitting authority between lookalike URLs.

On-page basics

  • Every service and industry page gets a unique title and meta description designed to win the click.
  • Primary pages ship with FAQ blocks and internal jumps so people can skim and still get what they came for.
  • We set up a simple blog hub and publish useful, non-fluffy content that backs up the sales pages.

Competitors at a glance

What most agencies do

  • They group work under a Services hub, with optional Industries or “Work” sections.
  • They talk about results but hide details on process, pricing, and what happens month by month.
  • They rarely show how web, SEO, and paid connect, so clients still have to guess what to do first.

Where Ovaro leans in

  • We keep the classic structure but add more practical detail on what we will ship in the first 90 days.
  • We publish simple starting ranges and scope examples so people can self-qualify before they book.
  • We show how tracking and reporting tie together, so decision makers can see how it all connects.

    First pages and why

    Web Design and Development pillar

    Most conversations start from “our site doesn’t match where we are now.” This pillar explains how Ovaro fixes that and pushes people into Discovery Calls, with clear examples of the work and process.

    SEO pillar

    For decision makers who care about inbound, the SEO pillar explains how strategy, content, and technical work fit together in a 90-day plan. Subpages like “SEO for Startups” let us speak directly to our main ICP.

    PPC and Paid Media pillar

    Founders who are already spending need a safe pair of hands, not more buzzwords. This page focuses on how we protect spend, clean up tracking, and test our way into reliable acquisition.

    Pricing and Why Ovaro

    Simple ranges, clear starting points, and a short “Why Ovaro” narrative help people understand fit fast. These pages keep serious buyers from bouncing to another tab before they talk to us.
