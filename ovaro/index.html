Ovaro. Web and strategy for brands that want to outperform.

Executive overview

Ovaro exists to be a calm, sharp partner for owners and small teams. The site does not need to be huge on day one. It needs a clear core, a simple structure and a way to grow into SEO, content and proof when the time is right. This deck is the working map.

How the site is framed

  • Start with a lean core: Home, Services, Portfolio, About and Contact. Enough for Ovaro to feel real when someone checks us out.
  • Add deeper service and industry pages once the core is stable and there is work to show.
  • Keep the full silo mapped from the start, but roll it out in phases so there is always a clear next move without pressure to ship everything at once.

What this plan covers

  • Positioning, service spine and who Ovaro is trying to talk to.
  • A phased site structure that starts small and can grow into services, industries and resources.
  • Competitor patterns and where Ovaro feels different without trying to be everything.
  • Keyword directions, internal linking and basic tracking that can be plugged in as growth becomes a focus.

Services and who we serve

Service spine

Eight buckets that cover what Ovaro can deliver as the agency grows.

  • Web and UI or UX custom sites, redesigns, landing pages and UX improvements.
  • Branding brand direction, visual identity and core assets.
  • SEO on page work, technical fixes and content planning.
  • PPC and paid ads mainly Google Ads, plus other channels when it makes sense.
  • Content and copy website copy, landing pages and longer pieces that support services.
  • Systems and integrations forms, booking, CRM, automation and the glue between tools.
  • Analytics and backend tracking, dashboards and basic reporting.
  • Support and consulting ongoing help, audits and advisory.

Early focus is on web, branding and simple tracking. The deeper stacks like SEO, PPC and complex systems sit on top of that and can be scoped per project.

Who this is designed for

  • New locations and studios physical spaces that are opening or relaunching and need a presence that matches the investment.
  • Healthcare and wellness practices physiotherapy, mental health and similar clinics that care about clear language and trust.
  • Local service businesses trades and professional services where people need fast clarity and easy ways to reach out.
  • Small brands and creators people who want a simple, serious home for their work outside of social platforms.

The plan stays flexible enough for more technical or online heavy brands, but it is grounded in real world service work and owners who want things to feel intentional, not flashy.

Target decision makers

Ovaro is built for people who decide where money and attention go. They are busy, they care about results and they do not want drama. They want someone who can handle detail without drowning them in it.

Age bands and roles

  • Main band: 28 to 52
    Owners, founders, partners, practice managers and marketing or operations leads who:
    • Control the budget or have a strong say in it.
    • Already feel that their site and brand are behind where the business is now.
    • Care more about clear work and honest communication than trends.
  • Secondary band: 22 to 27
    Early career operators and coordinators who:
    • Get asked to find a good partner for the website or marketing.
    • Do the research, shortlist options and sit in on calls.
    • Want structure, checklists and simple explanations so they look strong in front of the owner.
  • Additional band: 53 to 65
    Experienced owners and principals who:
    • Have run their business for years and know how they like to work.
    • Are fine investing once they trust the person and the plan.
    • Value stability, straight answers and one clear person to call.

What matters to them

  • Clarity simple breakdowns of what will be done, how it looks in real life and what is needed from their side.
  • Real proof before and after stories, short case notes and honest outcomes, not vague claims.
  • Clean next steps clear paths like a discovery call, a small direction phase and then a concrete build plan.
  • Fit with how they work respect for their time, realistic pacing and the ability to work with their current tools and team.
  • Confidence in the relationship one main contact, steady updates and a sense that Ovaro understands both online and offline parts of their business.

Most serious conversations start when something is changing. A new location, a rebrand or a site that no longer reflects reality. The site and offer should speak directly to that moment.

Competitors at a glance

How most agencies show up

  • Broad menus of SEO, PPC, web, content and lots of add ons.
  • Heavy focus on awards and scale, less on day to day experience once you sign.
  • Web builds and ongoing strategy often split across different pages and sometimes different teams.

Where Ovaro leans in

  • Keep a familiar structure but centre everything on a web and strategy spine.
  • Describe what happens in discovery, build and evolve, so people know what it feels like to work together.
  • Make sure services, industries and proof link to each other, so the site reflects how Ovaro actually works with clients.

    Phased site growth

    Phase 1. Core presence

    Make Ovaro feel real online with the minimum page set that still looks like a serious agency.

    • Home with clear Ovaro positioning and a simple overview of what we do.
    • Services hub with a short summary of the main service buckets.
    • Portfolio with a small, honest set of examples that match the work we want more of.
    • About with who is behind Ovaro and a short story about how we approach work.
    • Contact with a clean form and direct contact options.

    These are marked as stars in the silo view so it is easy to see what matters first.

    Phase 2. Depth and fit

    Once the core is live, start adding the pages that help people understand fit in more detail.

    • Dedicated service pages for website design, branding, SEO, PPC and content.
    • Industry pages for the patterns that appear first, like service businesses, clinics or professional firms.
    • Process and Why Ovaro pages that back up sales calls and follow ups.
    • Light resources like a blog or small guide set that support the main services.

    Phase 3. Authority and resources

    After there is a stable base of work and a clearer niche, add proof and tools that support bigger and more complex projects.

    • Case studies and result snapshots placed next to related service pages.
    • Playbooks, frameworks and guides that show how decisions are made.
    • Simple calculators or tools where they actually help, not just for show.
    • Deeper industry clusters and a more organised resources library if there is enough content.

    The silo view keeps the long term picture visible even if execution moves at a slower pace.

    First pages and why they matter

    Home

    The homepage sets the tone. It introduces Ovaro, states the web and strategy focus and gives people a quick view of services, proof and ways to get in touch.

    Services hub

    The services hub is a simple table of contents for what Ovaro does. It links into deeper service pages as they are created, but stays useful on its own even at the start.

    Portfolio

    A small, focused portfolio gives social proof even if the agency side is still young. Pieces can include improved old work, concept pieces and real client projects, as long as they are honest and aligned with what we want to sell.

    About and Contact

    The about page explains who runs Ovaro, what we care about and how projects normally flow. Contact gives people a clean, low friction way to reach out by form, email or call.

    How engagements usually work

    Discovery and direction

    Before anyone jumps into a full build, there is a short discovery and direction phase. The point is to understand the offer, constraints and role of the site.

    • Clarify services, audience, positioning and what needs to be true for this to feel like a win.
    • Rough out the sitemap and decide what is needed now and what can wait.
    • Set an initial brand direction so web, print and space can move in the same direction if needed.

    Build and evolve

    After direction is clear, the work moves into build and then ongoing improvement.

    • Design and build of the first core pages and any agreed service or industry pages.
    • Hook up tracking, forms, booking and other basics that make the site useful in real life.
    • Add more depth over time as patterns show up, instead of throwing everything in at once.

    Brand and web work can inform interior choices and offline branding. Legal, incorporation, leases and finance are separate work streams and only join this plan if they are clearly scoped.

    Client types and how they usually find us

    Ideal early clients

    • New locations studios, clinics and other spaces that are opening or changing direction.
    • Owner led services businesses where the owner is close to the work and to the buying decisions.
    • Small brands and creators people who want a serious, clean home for their work that they can grow into.

    These clients usually need branding, a website and some basic systems at the same time. That matches how Ovaro is set up.

    Where work comes from first

    • Referrals and intros from people already connected to Ovaro.
    • Owners and managers in the middle of a move, a new lease or a relaunch.
    • Light use of platforms when it is useful, for very specific offers like website redesigns for service businesses.
    • Direct outreach backed by a site that feels calm, serious and clear.

    Over time, SEO, content and maybe paid campaigns can carry more of the load. At the start, trust and direct conversations matter most.

    How to use this

    • Pair each service page with a small cluster of related terms, not just one main phrase.
    • Let real work and actual client types decide which clusters to chase first.
    • Write pages that explain what we do and why, then tune headings and copy to support these terms.

    What to watch when SEO matters more

    • Clicks and impressions for these terms in Search Console.
    • Sessions and behaviour on the mapped service, industry and resource pages.
    • Leads and calls that can be tied back to search, not just raw rankings.

    Internal linking and measurement

    Internal linking rules

    • Home links clearly into services, key service pages, portfolio and contact.
    • Service pages link to related industries and any case notes that show the work in context.
    • Industry pages link back to the services that solve problems for that segment and to a relevant contact or enquiry path.
    • Blog and resource pieces link up to the main services they support instead of pointing only to the homepage.

    Analytics and technical basics

    • Set up Google Analytics 4 and Tag Manager for form submits, click to call, email clicks and booking events.
    • Verify the main domain in Search Console and submit a single XML sitemap.
    • Keep one primary domain and protocol, with HTTPS forced and redirects from any old or alternate routes.
    • Use simple events or goals to track key journeys like Home to Services to Contact or Home to Service to Portfolio to Contact.

    The goal is to see what people actually do on the site, not just how it looks. That feedback guides the next phase of pages and improvements.
